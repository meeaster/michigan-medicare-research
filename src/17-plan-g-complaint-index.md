## Plan G — NAIC Complaint Index (Member Satisfaction)

**Source:** National Association of Insurance Commissioners (NAIC) complaint data for 2022–2024 where available, plus secondary summaries from NerdWallet, ValuePenguin, MedicareGuide.com, and MedicareCoverGuide. The NAIC complaint index measures how many complaints a carrier receives relative to its market share — **1.0 = expected for a company its size**, below 1.0 = fewer complaints, above 1.0 = more complaints. **Lower is better.** Values marked "estimated" are directional placeholders from secondary research, not verified NAIC company-level figures.

### Why This Matters for Choosing a Plan G

Loss ratio tells you how much of your premium dollar is paid out as claims. The complaint index tells you how painful the experience is *while you're filing those claims*. A carrier with a great loss ratio but bad complaint index is one where you might have to call them five times to get a claim paid. A carrier with mediocre loss ratio but great complaint index is one where things just work.

### Key Findings

**🟢 Best complaint profiles (lowest friction when filing claims):**

- **Mutual of Omaha** (Omaha Supplemental) — 52% fewer complaints than expected. Best in the entire Plan G set. NerdWallet's #1 pick.
- **State Farm** — 49% fewer complaints. NerdWallet's top pick overall for 2026. Strong digital tools, minimal member friction.
- **BCBS Michigan (state-specific)** — 30% fewer complaints than BCBS national average. JD Power #1 among all BCBS affiliates (675/1000 in 2025 study).
- **AARP / UnitedHealthcare** — Below the industry average despite being the largest Medigap carrier. Strong digital tools, 24/7 customer support, large member-services infrastructure.

**🟡 Average complaint profiles:**

- Most MI-domestic carriers (Priority Health, HAP, McLaren/Blue Care Network, Everence, USAA) cluster around the 1.0 industry average.
- Aetna/Continental Life: 36% more complaints than average. Pricing is also 58% higher than the cheapest Plan G.
- United American: 13% more complaints AND 90% higher Plan G premium than cheapest. Two strikes.
- Medico: 29% more complaints, plus above-average loss ratio (94.9%), plus 30% higher premium than top companies. Multiple sources flag for unclear coverage details and high rate of claims issues.

**🔴 Worst complaint profiles (consider avoiding unless price is critical):**

- **Humana (KY) and Humana Achieve** — 228% more complaints than average. NerdWallet: "Members file complaints about Humana Medigap policies 228% more often than the average for all Medigap companies." Pricing is also 54% higher than the cheapest Plan G.
- **AFLAC** — 262% more complaints than average. 1.5/5 stars on BBB. New to Medigap market (started 2022). Even NerdWallet says "Aflac isn't one of my top picks."
- **Transamerica** — not in the current scraped Michigan Plan G set, but useful as an industry cautionary example: secondary sources report a very high complaint index and frequent service complaints.
- **American Benefit Life** — 107.9% loss ratio (paying out more than it collects) and high complaint volume. **Avoid.**

### Important Caveats

- **NAIC data is at the parent-company level**, not the individual product. Continental Life (Aetna) gets Aetna's 1.36 index even though Continental's specific customers may have a different experience.
- **The complaint index doesn't reflect the *severity* of complaints** — a 0.5 index could mean 100 complaints about billing questions (low severity) or 50 complaints about claim denials (high severity). For severity, read individual user reviews on Consumer Affairs and BBB.
- **Smaller carriers with low complaint volume** (e.g., Bankers Fidelity, Nassau Life) can swing wildly on the index year-over-year. A single bad batch of claims can move their index from 0.5 to 1.8. Trust 3-year averages more than any single year.
- **Michigan Farm Bureau** has no public NAIC complaint data because its Medigap block is administered by Cincinnati Equitable (only 6 covered lives in the NAIC report). Farm Bureau's home/auto insurance has a separate, more robust complaint profile — see BBB.
- **The "fewer complaints" can be misleading** — a carrier with 5,000 members and 1 complaint (0.5 index) may have worse real-world service than a carrier with 100,000 members and 50 complaints (0.5 index). The index is normalized to market share, but the underlying absolute numbers also matter.
