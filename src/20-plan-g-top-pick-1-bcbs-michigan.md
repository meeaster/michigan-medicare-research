## Plan G — Top Recommendation #1: Blue Cross Blue Shield of Michigan

**The "default Michigan pick"** — best balance of premium, rate stability, financial strength, and member satisfaction of any Plan G carrier in the state. The recommendation that should anchor a Michigan resident's short list.

### The One-Line Summary

> **BCBS Michigan Plan G costs $191/mo at age 65, has taken 6.5% rate increases for two consecutive years, holds an A (Excellent) financial strength rating with 86.3% loss ratio, and appears to be one of the largest Michigan Medigap carriers.** No other Plan G in the state combines this Michigan-domestic profile with these publicly documented stability signals.

### Premium & Tiers

BCBS Michigan offers Plan G in two tiers at the same $191/mo for a 65-year-old female non-smoker in Michigan:

| Tier | Monthly | Underwriting | Who It Targets |
|------|--------:|--------------|----------------|
| Guaranteed Issue | $191 | None required | Applicants outside their Medigap Open Enrollment window with qualifying health events |
| Non-Guaranteed Issue | $191 | Standard health questions | Applicants within their 6-month Medigap Open Enrollment window (best for most 65-year-olds) |

**Pricing method:** Attained-age (premium rises as you age, on top of inflation-driven rate increases).

### Why This Is the Top Pick

**1. Stable recent Michigan-specific rate increases among major carriers.**

BCBS Michigan has filed **6.5% in 2024 and 6.5% in 2025** for Plan G in Michigan — and has not yet filed a 2026 Michigan rate increase as of April 2026. For comparison, the Plan G industry average in 2025 was 12.7%. Among the major/top-pick carriers with recent Michigan-specific filings found, BCBS Michigan's 6.5% is the lowest. State Farm (7.4%), Mutual of Omaha (8.8%, varies by state), and AARP/UHC (10.3%) are higher. AFLAC took 22.0% in 2026, Continental Life (Aetna) took 22.0% in 2025, and McLaren took 14.5% in 2026.

**2. Healthy loss ratio of 86.3%.**

The 2024 NAIC Medicare Supplement Loss Ratio Report shows BCBS Michigan's parent entity (BCBS of MI Mutual Insurance Co, cocode 54291) at **86.3% loss ratio on $313M in direct premiums earned**. This is in the "healthy" 75–90% range — meaning 86 cents of every premium dollar goes back out to policyholders as claims, while 14 cents covers administration, commissions, taxes, and (modest) profit. The carrier is not bleeding cash (like American Benefit Life at 107.9% or Humana Achieve at 102.4%), and it is not over-charging (like Bankers Fidelity at 71.9%).

**3. Below-average complaint index — specific to BCBS Michigan (not just BCBS national).**

Secondary summaries of NAIC complaint data show BCBS of MI Mutual Insurance Co at a complaint index of approximately **0.70 — 30% fewer complaints than expected for a company its size**. This is the state-specific Michigan entity, not the national BCBS collective. Michigan's BCBS affiliate also scored **675/1000 in the JD Power 2025 Medicare Advantage Study**, the highest of any BCBS affiliate in that Medicare Advantage study; that is indirect brand/service evidence, not Medigap-specific complaint data.

**4. Largest MI-domestic Medigap block (~27% market share).**

BCBS Michigan is inferred to be the anonymous #2 carrier in the UHC market report, with an estimated 102,945 Michigan Medigap members (27.47% of the state's 374,795 total). The report does not name that carrier, so this should be treated as a high-confidence inference, not a confirmed fact. If correct, the size of BCBS Michigan's MI-domiciled block means: (a) rate filings are reviewed with specific attention to Michigan's market; (b) the carrier has scale advantages on administration and claims handling; (c) closed-block risk is much lower than for tiny carriers.

**5. A (Excellent) financial strength rating, MI-domestic regulatory oversight.**

AM Best rates BCBS Michigan at **A (Excellent)** as of December 4, 2025. The rating carries a **Negative outlook** — meaning AM Best is considering a potential downgrade — but the rating itself remains in the "Secure" range. The Negative outlook reflects non-Medigap business (group health, Medicare Advantage) under margin pressure, not the Medigap block, which is the most stable line of business the company writes. A rating of A- or better qualifies as "Secure" for long-term insurance products.

- ✅ **0.70 secondary-reported NAIC complaint index** (30% below expected) — JD Power #1 BCBS affiliate in a Medicare Advantage study

### Cons

- ⚠️ **AM Best Negative outlook** (Dec 2025) — possibility of future downgrade, though Medigap operations are stable
- ⚠️ **Mid-range premium** ($191) — not the cheapest. Michigan Farm Bureau is $113, State Farm is $149, Mutual of Omaha is $220, AARP/UHC is $182
- ⚠️ **Two-tier pricing structure** — Guaranteed Issue and Non-Guaranteed Issue are priced identically at age 65 but the GI tier may have different long-term trajectory
- ⚠️ **2016 Legacy Medigap shock** — In 2016, BCBS Michigan raised Legacy Medigap Plan C rates by $48–$177/mo for ~200,000 seniors. Plan C and Plan G are different products, but the event is a reminder that BCBS MI *can* file large increases when justified by block experience
- ⚠️ **No household discount** documented in the Plan G tiers shown
- ⚠️ **One phone line for both tiers** — customer service call volume is high due to large block size

### Best For

- Is willing to pay ~$80/mo more than the cheapest Plan G (Michigan Farm Bureau $113) for proven stability
- Values "Michigan-domestic" regulatory protection over nationwide scale
- Has a qualifying guaranteed-issue event if outside Open Enrollment, or can pass underwriting
- Plans to keep this Plan G policy for 10+ years (the longer you hold, the more the 6.5% rate stability pays off vs higher-increase carriers)
- Wants a carrier that will *still be there* in 20 years with predictable rate increases

- **The price-sensitive buyer** — Michigan Farm Bureau ($113) or Cigna HealthSpring ($173) cost less today, even if lifetime cost is higher

- **The buyer in poor health** — BCBS Michigan's Guaranteed Issue tier is more expensive in the long run for those who can pass underwriting elsewhere

### Caveats

- **The 6.5% recent rate increases may not continue.** Two years is a short history. If BCBS Michigan's block experience worsens (e.g., due to higher Medicare Part B deductible or changes in claim patterns), the carrier could file for higher increases in 2027 or 2028. The 6.5% rate is *likely* to continue, not *guaranteed* to continue.
- **The Negative outlook from AM Best is a real signal.** If the outlook resolves to a downgrade, BCBS Michigan drops to A- (still "Secure" but at the bottom of the band). Watch for AM Best rating actions in 2026-2027.
- **The 2016 Legacy Medigap shock is a historical lesson, not a forecast.** Plan C and Plan G are priced and experience-rated differently. But it demonstrates that BCBS Michigan will raise rates significantly when its block requires it.
- **"Largest MI-domestic block" doesn't mean lowest increases in absolute terms.** A large block means rate filings are scrutinized, but it also means small changes in block experience can drive larger absolute premium dollar changes. A 6.5% increase on $191/mo is $12.42/mo more — the same percentage on a $1,000/mo policy would be $65/mo.
- **For a 65-year-old turning 65 today, the 6-month Medigap Open Enrollment window is the only time BCBS Michigan cannot deny you or charge more for pre-existing conditions.** Outside that window, if you want to switch TO BCBS Michigan from another carrier, you may have to answer health questions and could be declined or charged a higher tier.
