## Medigap Switching Rights in Michigan

**This is the most important section of this document to read before you sign up for a plan.** Choosing the wrong Medigap carrier can lock you into 5, 10, or 20 years of premium increases with no easy way out.

### The Core Rule: Federal 6-Month Open Enrollment Period

When you first enroll in **Medicare Part B** (typically at age 65), you have a **6-month Medigap Open Enrollment Period (OEP)**. During this window:

- **No medical underwriting.** Insurers must accept you regardless of your health.
- **No rate-ups** for pre-existing conditions.
- **Limited pre-existing-condition waiting-period risk.** If you have sufficient prior creditable coverage, the insurer generally cannot make you wait for pre-existing-condition coverage; without creditable coverage, a waiting period may still apply.
- You can buy **any Medigap plan** sold in Michigan.

**After this 6-month window closes**, in most states (including Michigan), insurers can:
- **Deny your application** based on health
- **Charge you a higher premium** (rate-up)
- **Impose a 6-month waiting period** for pre-existing conditions

This is why insurance brokers push you to "decide during your OEP." Once it closes, you may be stuck with your current carrier for life.

### Does Michigan Have a Medigap Birthday Rule?

**❌ No. Michigan does not have a Medigap birthday rule.**

As of June 2026, only the following states have a Medigap birthday rule (each with different terms):

| State | Birthday Rule | Window | Can Upgrade Plans? |
|---|---|---|---|
| California | ✅ | 60 days after birthday | No (equal or lesser) |
| Oregon | ✅ | 60 days after birthday | No (equal or lesser) |
| Idaho | ✅ | 63 days after birthday | No (equal or lesser) |
| Illinois | ✅ | 45 days before + 30 days after birthday | No (equal or lesser) |
| Nevada | ✅ | 60 days after birthday | No (equal or lesser) |
| Louisiana | ✅ | 30 days before + 63 days after policy anniversary | No (equal or lesser) |
| Missouri | ✅ | 30 days before + 30 days after policy anniversary | No (equal or lesser) |
| Maryland | ✅ | 30 days after birthday | No (equal or lesser) |
| Rhode Island | ✅ | 30 days after birthday | No (equal or lesser) |
| New York | ✅ | Year-round | ✅ Yes (any plan) |
| Connecticut | ✅ | Year-round | ✅ Yes (any plan) |
| Massachusetts | ✅ | Year-round | ✅ Yes (any plan) |
| Maine | ✅ | Year-round | ✅ Yes (any plan) |
| Vermont | ✅ | Year-round | ✅ Yes (any plan) |

**Michigan's status:** No birthday rule, no annual Medigap OEP, no year-round guaranteed issue. After the initial 6-month OEP closes, **you must pass medical underwriting to switch to a new Medigap carrier**.

### Pending Michigan Legislation

**Senate Bill 1143 of 2024** (introduced November 26, 2024) would create a Medigap switching right in Michigan. Separate 2025 proposals have also been discussed. As of June 2026, no Michigan Medigap birthday-rule bill has been enacted.

**If passed**, the bill would likely:
- Allow Michigan Medigap enrollees to switch to the **same plan letter** (Plan G → Plan G, etc.) with a different carrier
- Apply only during a limited window around the policyholder's birthday
- Prohibit medical underwriting during the window
- **Not** allow plan upgrades (Plan N → Plan G)
- Possibly also expand under-65 access and require more favorable premiums for disabled enrollees

**Until a bill passes, plan as though the birthday rule won't arrive in time for your next enrollment decision.** Sources: [Michigan Legislature SB 1143 of 2024](https://www.legislature.mi.gov/Bills/Bill?ObjectName=2024-SB-1143), [Eligry Medicare Solutions](https://eligry.com/michigan-medicare-supplement-underwriting-rules-2026-eligry/), [Senior65 Ultimate Guide](https://www.senior65.com/medicare/article/medigap-birthday-rule-ultimate-state-guide).

### What You Can Still Do After Your OEP Closes

You have **limited options** for switching Medigap plans outside the OEP in Michigan:

**1. Federal Guaranteed Issue Rights (Trial Rights)**

You can buy certain Medigap plans without underwriting in these specific situations:

- **You joined a Medicare Advantage (MA) plan when first eligible at 65, and you want to switch back to Original Medicare + Medigap within 12 months.** This is the "MA trial right" — but you can only use it once.
- **You had Medigap, then joined MA for the first time, then want to switch back to Medigap within 12 months.**
- **Your Medigap insurer goes bankrupt or ends your policy.**
- **You move out of your Medigap insurer's service area** (less relevant for Medigap since it has no networks, but applies to Medicare SELECT plans).
- **You lose employer-sponsored health coverage** (group health plan that supplemented Medicare).

In these cases, you generally have **63 days** from the triggering event to buy certain Medigap plans without underwriting. Michigan DIFS lists standardized Plans **A, B, C, F, high-deductible F, K, or L** for guaranteed-issue rights, with MACRA reassignments for people newly eligible for Medicare on or after Jan 1, 2020: **C → D, F → G, and high-deductible F → high-deductible G**. **Plan N is not included** in the standard federal/Michigan guaranteed-issue set.

**2. Michigan-Specific Under-65 Rights**

Michigan DIFS says that if you are under age 65 and on Medicare, your Medicare Supplement choices are generally limited to **Plan A or Plan C**, from a limited number of insurers required to offer those plans to under-65 beneficiaries. For people newly eligible for Medicare on or after Jan 1, 2020, **Plan C is reassigned as Plan D** because MACRA prohibits newly eligible beneficiaries from buying Medigap coverage for the Part B deductible. Higher premiums may be allowed. Verify current under-65 availability directly with Michigan DIFS or MMAP before relying on this right.

**3. Medical Underwriting (the default path)**

This is how most Michigan Medigap switches happen. The new carrier reviews:
- Your health history (typically questions about the past 2-5 years)
- Your prescription medications
- Tobacco use
- Height/weight
- Any pending surgeries or hospitalizations
- Common conditions: heart disease, cancer (within 5 years), diabetes, COPD, kidney disease, etc.

You may be:
- **Approved at standard rates** — same as anyone else your age
- **Approved with a rate-up** — 10-50% higher than standard
- **Approved with a pre-existing condition waiting period** — up to 6 months (some carriers waive this)
- **Declined** — no offer at all

**Tip:** Some carriers (like BCBS Michigan with their "Blue to Blue" program) allow internal switches between their own Medigap plans with relaxed underwriting. If you have a BCBS Michigan Medigap policy, call them at 877-469-2583 to ask about internal switches before trying to switch carriers.

### The Trap: Closed-Block Carriers

If your carrier **stops accepting new Medigap enrollees** (Manhattan Life, ACE/Chubb, and Allstate have all done this in 2025), your block becomes a "closed block." Without new young/healthy members coming in, the average age of the block rises, claims rise, and the carrier files for large rate increases. **One Chubb broker reported 80 customers in Illinois hit with 45% increases** in a single filing.

If you are currently enrolled with a closed-block carrier in Michigan, your options are:
1. **Apply for medical underwriting elsewhere now**, while you may still be in decent health
2. **Wait for Michigan's birthday rule to pass** (uncertain timeline)
3. **Stay put and accept the rate increases** (often the worst option)

### What to Do Right Now

**If you are still in your 6-month Medigap OEP (turning 65, just enrolled in Part B):**

1. **Don't pick based on premium alone.** Pick based on:
   - 3-5 year rate-increase history in Michigan
   - NAIC complaint index
   - Loss ratio
   - Whether the carrier has a history of closing blocks
2. **Compare at least 3 carriers' Plan G** (this document covers all 41).
3. **Verify the carrier is committed to the Michigan Medigap market** for the long term.

**If you are outside your OEP and unhappy with your current carrier:**

1. **Get quotes from 3-5 alternative carriers** based on your specific age, ZIP, and health.
2. **Apply to the alternative carrier** — they will underwrite you. If approved, you have 30 days to "free-look" the new policy and cancel the old one.
3. **Do not cancel your current policy** until the new one is approved and in force.
4. **Be aware** that switching means you lose any rate-stability history you have with your current carrier.

**If you are approaching age 65 and considering Medicare Advantage instead of Medigap:**

1. **Understand** that once you choose MA, you can usually only get a Medigap policy with medical underwriting after the first 12 months. (Trial right exception.)
2. **You can change from MA back to Medigap** during the 12-month trial right, but you can only use this once.

### Free Michigan Counseling Resources

- **MMAP (Michigan Medicare Assistance Program)** — Michigan's State Health Insurance Assistance Program (SHIP). Free, unbiased counseling for Michigan Medicare beneficiaries. **1-800-803-7174** or [mmapinc.org](https://mmapinc.org).
- **Michigan Department of Insurance and Financial Services (DIFS)** — **1-877-999-6442** or [michigan.gov/difs](https://www.michigan.gov/difs). Regulates Medigap rate filings, handles complaints, can answer policy questions.
- **Medicare.gov Plan Finder** — [medicare.gov/plan-compare](https://www.medicare.gov/plan-compare). The official source for Michigan Medigap pricing.
- **1-800-MEDICARE** — **1-800-633-4227**. 24/7 help line for general Medicare questions.

### What MMAP and DIFS Will Not Do

MMAP and DIFS counselors:
- ✅ Can explain Medigap rules and how they apply to you
- ✅ Can help you understand rate-increase letters
- ✅ Can file complaints on your behalf with carriers
- ❌ **Cannot** recommend specific carriers or plans (they are unbiased)
- ❌ **Cannot** sell you insurance (they don't take commissions)
- ❌ **Cannot** negotiate your premium

For carrier-specific advice, you'll need a **licensed insurance agent** (who is paid on commission by the carrier) or a **fee-based Medicare advisor** (who is paid by you).

### Important Caveats

- **The "Blue to Blue" internal switch program is BCBS-Michigan-specific** and can change at any time. Always verify current terms with BCBS Michigan directly.
- **Federal guaranteed issue rights have strict 63-day windows.** Miss the window and you lose the right.
- **Medical underwriting decisions are made by each individual carrier** and can vary significantly. A decline from one carrier doesn't mean another will decline.
- **Smoking, weight, and certain health conditions** can each trigger rate-ups or declines. Be honest on the application — material misrepresentation gives the carrier a basis to rescind your policy later.
- **Pending Michigan legislation is not a guarantee.** S.B.1143 could pass, could pass in modified form, or could die in committee. Don't make a financial decision based on a "maybe."
