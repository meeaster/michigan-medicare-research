## Plan G — Pros & Cons of Each Carrier (Selection Guide)

This section distills the loss-ratio, complaint-index, rate-increase, AM Best, and market-share data from the preceding sections into a **per-carrier pros-and-cons summary** for every company selling Plan G in Michigan. Use it as the final cross-check before you call for a quote.

**How to read each entry:**

- The **at-a-glance line** shows: 2026 Plan G premium (age 65, female, non-smoker, Michigan) · AM Best financial-strength rating · 2024 NAIC loss ratio · NAIC complaint index (lower is better; 1.0 = average) · most recent known rate increase.
- The **verdict tag** uses the same composite scale as the *Financial Strength, Market Share & Long-Term Cost* section: 🟢 Strong · 🟡 Moderate · 🟠 Caution · 🔴 Avoid · ⚠️ Unproven.
- Carriers are ordered by **2026 Plan G premium, cheapest first** — the same order as the Plan G premium table.
- Several carriers sell multiple tiers (Preferred / Standard / Tier 1-3). The pros and cons apply to the carrier as a whole; the premium shown is the entry tier unless noted.
- The three carriers with dedicated deep-dive sections — **BCBS Michigan, Mutual of Omaha, and AARP/UnitedHealthcare** — are summarized here in brief; see their own sections for the full analysis.

> **One caveat that applies to all 41 policies:** Plan G benefits are identical by law across every carrier. The *only* things that differ are premium, pricing method, financial strength, claims-paying friction, and rate-increase trajectory. You are not choosing better coverage — you are choosing the company most likely to keep that coverage affordable and pay cleanly for the next 20 years.

### 1. Michigan Farm Bureau Health Plans — $113/mo · ⚠️ Unproven

*At a glance: $113 · AM Best A (P&C parent; Farm Bureau Life A) · loss ratio not publicly available (block too small) · complaint index not reported · no public MI rate history.*

**Pros:**
- ✅ **Cheapest Plan G in Michigan** — $36/mo less than the next option, ~$78/mo below BCBS Michigan
- ✅ **Michigan-domestic** carrier with a recognizable in-state brand
- ✅ Underlying Farm Bureau Life entity is **A (Excellent)** rated; the P&C side is well-established in Michigan
- ✅ Attained-age pricing starts very low for a 65-year-old

**Cons:**
- ❌ **No public loss ratio, complaint index, or rate-increase history** — the Medigap block is administered through Cincinnati Equitable with only ~6 covered lives in the NAIC report
- ❌ **Tiny, unproven block** — real "ghost rate / closed-block" risk; the carrier's long-term commitment to Medigap is unclear
- ❌ The cheapest premium today tells you nothing about the 5- and 10-year trajectory, which is the number that actually matters
- ❌ Cheapest-rate availability varies by ZIP and age bracket

### Bottom Line — Shortlist by Buyer Type

| If you want… | Carrier | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **The strongest all-around Michigan default** | **BCBS Michigan** ($191) | MI-domestic, 6.5% increases, healthy LR, low complaints, A-rated |
| **The highest carrier quality (best claims experience)** | **Mutual of Omaha** ($220) | A+ Superior, 0.48 complaint index (best), #2 nationally |
| **The lowest premium from a strong carrier** | **State Farm** ($149) | A+, 0.51 complaint index, 82.7% LR, modest increases |
| **Maximum scale & predictability** | **AARP / UnitedHealthcare** ($182) | Largest MI/U.S. block, audited history — but watch 12–17% recent increases |
| **Highest financial strength (if eligible)** | **USAA** ($179) | A++ (highest), 83.9% LR — military families only |
| **The absolute cheapest (eyes open)** | **Michigan Farm Bureau** ($113) | Lowest premium, but unproven block with no public data |

**Carriers to approach with caution or avoid for a 20-year hold:** American Benefit Life (107.9% LR), GPM Health and Life (B++, $373), AFLAC (3.62 complaints, 22% increase), Humana (3.28 complaints, unprofitable Achieve block), and Nassau Life (B++). For all of these, the cheap or convenient entry point is outweighed by financial-strength, complaint, or rate-trajectory risk.

> Cross-reference every carrier here against the **Loss Ratio**, **Complaint Index**, **Rate Increase**, and **Financial Strength / Market Share** sections before deciding — and remember that for an attained-age policy, the rate-increase trajectory matters more than today's premium.
