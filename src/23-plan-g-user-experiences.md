## Plan G — Real User Experiences (2024–2026)

This section compiles first-person reports from Medigap enrollees in Michigan and other states, sourced from public forums (AARP Online Community, Bogleheads, City-Data, Early-Retirement.org, Consumer Affairs), review aggregators, and news articles. **These are not Michigan-specific in every case** — UHC rate-hike patterns are similar across states — but they illustrate how the rate-increase percentages from the previous section actually feel at the kitchen table.

### Class Action Lawsuit — Sacchi v. AARP, et al. (filed February 2026; reported March 2026)

A proposed nationwide class action was filed in **U.S. District Court for the District of New Jersey** in February 2026 by plaintiff John Sacchi and was reported by class-action news outlets in March 2026. The complaint alleges:

> *"For decades, [UnitedHealthcare] has violated the subject promises to pay for medically necessary healthcare not paid by Medicare. [UHC] intends to continue systematically denying such claims by citing a phantom, non-existent condition that appears nowhere in the policies' subject Certificate of Insurance."*

The "phantom condition" allegedly used as a denial basis: **the healthcare provider must "participate in or accept Medicare"** for the claim to be covered. Plaintiffs argue this requirement is **nowhere in the Certificate of Insurance** but is being invoked to deny claims.

**Lead plaintiff's case:** John Sacchi, a senior citizen and stroke survivor, underwent medically necessary surgery to remove cancerous tissue from his eyelid and a follow-up corrective procedure. After being told by an AARP representative on a recorded call that the surgery would be covered, UHC denied the claim — leaving him with nearly **$8,000 in unpaid medical bills**.

**Class sought:** All individuals who, since 2014, were AARP members and policyholders of an AARP Medicare Supplement plan from UnitedHealthcare and received one or more denials of reimbursement for medically necessary healthcare on the basis that the provider did not participate in or accept Medicare.

**AARP's alleged role:** The complaint alleges AARP receives royalty fees from UHC for the use of the AARP brand on these Medigap plans, making AARP a knowing participant in the alleged scheme. Public reports show AARP's total annual royalty revenue is about $1 billion, mostly health-related, but current UHC-specific amounts are not fully disclosed and AARP disputes critics' characterization of the relationship.

**Status as of June 2026:** Case is in early procedural stages. No class certification yet. UHC has not publicly admitted liability.

**Source:** [classaction.org](https://www.classaction.org/news/class-action-lawsuit-says-aarp-unitedhealthcare-fraudulently-deny-medicare-supplement-claims), [topclassactions.com](https://topclassactions.com/lawsuit-settlements/lawsuit-news/aarp-unitedhealthcare-class-action-alleges-wrongful-medicare-supplement-claim-denials/), [classactionu.org](https://classactionu.org/our-news/aarp-and-unitedhealthcare-hit-with-class-action-lawsuit-over-alleged-medicare-supplement-fraud/)

This sentiment captures what 10%+ annual Medigap increases feel like on a fixed income: the headline rate increase is news, but the *real* story is what it does to disposable income after the COLA and Social Security deductions.

### Important Caveats

- **This section is anecdotal.** A few angry forum posters don't prove that every UHC customer is unhappy. But the *volume* and *consistency* of complaints about AARP/UHC and AFLAC in 2024-2026 is a meaningful signal.
- **The most-membership carriers get the most complaints.** UHC has 3.8M members. AFLAC has tens of thousands. Comparing absolute complaint counts between them is unfair — that's why the NAIC complaint *index* is more useful.
- **Some complaints are about Medicare Advantage, not Medigap.** I tried to filter for Medigap-specific complaints. The line between "AARP/UHC Medigap" and "AARP/UHC Medicare Advantage" is blurred in some forum posts.
- **Forum users may not represent the typical customer.** People who post on forums are more likely to have had a strong experience (positive or negative) than the average member.
- **Michigan-specific data is limited** because Michigan doesn't have a high-traffic dedicated Medigap forum. Most of the user-experience data here is from AARP Community (which has Michigan posters) and other state-based sources.
- **The class action lawsuit is an allegation, not a proven fact.** UHC has not admitted liability, and the case is in early stages. But the existence of the lawsuit (and the 26-page detail in the complaint) is itself a signal that something unusual may be happening with AARP/UHC Medigap claims.
