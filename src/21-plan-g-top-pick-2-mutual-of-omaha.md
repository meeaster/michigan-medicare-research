## Plan G — Top Recommendation #2: Mutual of Omaha (Omaha Supplemental Insurance Company)

**The "highest carrier quality" pick** — strongest AM Best rating among the affordable Plan G carriers, lowest NAIC complaint index in the entire Plan G set, and #2 nationally by enrollment. The recommendation for buyers willing to pay ~$30/mo more than the mid-priced options to get the best risk-adjusted quality.

### The One-Line Summary

> **Mutual of Omaha Plan G costs $220/mo at age 65, holds an A+ (Superior) financial strength rating with 0.48 NAIC complaint index (52% fewer complaints than expected — best in the set), and is the #2 Medigap carrier nationally with 1.3M members.** No other Plan G in Michigan combines A+ Superior with the lowest complaint index and the scale of a top-2 national carrier.

**Pricing method:** Attained-age (premium rises as you age, on top of inflation-driven rate increases).

**Important note on Mutual of Omaha's corporate structure:** The "Mutual of Omaha" brand encompasses 5 separate NAIC-registered entities (Mutual of Omaha Insurance Co, Omaha Insurance Company, United of Omaha Life Insurance Co, United World Life Insurance Co, and Omaha Supplemental Insurance Co). The Plan G product sold in Michigan is underwritten by **Omaha Supplemental Insurance Company** specifically. Loss ratios differ across entities — the Omaha Supplemental entity is at 97.5% (paying out close to actual cost), while other Mutual of Omaha entities range from 77% to 100%. Be aware that some "Mutual of Omaha" marketing material refers to the parent mutual company (which has a different loss ratio).

### Why This Is the Top Pick

**1. A+ (Superior) financial strength rating — one notch above the rest of the top picks.**

AM Best rates Mutual of Omaha (parent entity) at **A+ (Superior) with a Stable outlook** as of March 13, 2025. This is one notch above BCBS Michigan (A) and AARP/UHC (A, downgraded from A+ in August 2025). The A+ rating is in the second-highest tier of AM Best's 16-level scale and is described as "Superior" ability to meet ongoing insurance obligations. For a 20-year Medigap policy, this is the rating band you want.

**2. Lowest NAIC complaint index in the entire Plan G set: 0.48.**

NAIC complaint data for the Omaha Supplemental Insurance Co entity shows a complaint index of approximately **0.48 — meaning Mutual of Omaha Plan G enrollees file 52% fewer complaints than expected for a company its size**. This is the lowest in the entire 41-Plan-G set in Michigan. For comparison: BCBS Michigan is 0.70 (30% fewer), AARP/UHC is <1.0 (good), State Farm is 0.51 (49% fewer), AFLAC is 3.62 (262% *more* — among the worst), and Humana is 3.28 (228% more). The 0.48 figure is NerdWallet's #1 pick for the entire Medigap industry in 2026.

What "0.48 complaint index" means in practice: when you call Mutual of Omaha about a claim, the call gets answered promptly, the claim is processed without multiple follow-ups, and the member services team resolves the issue on the first or second call. Carriers with high complaint indices (Humana 3.28, AFLAC 3.62) routinely have members reporting that claims are denied without clear explanation, customer service takes 45+ minutes to answer, or bills are sent in error and not corrected promptly.

**3. 97.5% loss ratio — paying out close to actual cost.**

The 2024 NAIC Medicare Supplement Loss Ratio Report shows Omaha Supplemental Insurance Co at **97.5% loss ratio on $162M in direct premiums earned**. This is in the 90-100% "thin margin" zone. It means that for every $1.00 Mutual of Omaha collects in Plan G premiums, $0.975 is paid back out as claims. The carrier is essentially pricing close to break-even on this block.

This is **not a bad sign** — it's actually a sign of fair pricing. A 97.5% loss ratio means Mutual of Omaha is not subsidizing the parent company's other businesses (unlike Bankers Fidelity at 71.9%, where the low loss ratio may indicate over-charging or block run-off) and is not making outsized profits on Medigap (unlike American Benefit Life at 107.9%, which is selling below cost and will inevitably need to raise rates).

The trade-off: 97.5% loss ratio means Mutual of Omaha will file rate increases when claim costs rise (which is why 2025 saw an 8.8% increase). But the increases are *justified* by claim experience, not by profit motives.

**4. 8.8% rate increase in 2025 — moderate, not volatile.**

Mutual of Omaha's Plan G rate increase in 2025 was **8.8% (March-May 2025, varies by state)**. For 2024, it was 7.9% (April 2024). These are moderate increases — below the Plan G industry average of 12.7% in 2025 and the AARP/UHC recent increases of 10.3-17.7%. Mutual of Omaha has not yet filed a 2026 Michigan rate increase (as of April 2026 data). The 8.8% recent rate is consistent with a 97.5% loss ratio — the carrier is keeping pace with claim inflation, not playing catch-up after years of underpricing.

**5. #2 Medigap carrier nationally — 1.3M members.**

Mutual of Omaha is the #2 Medigap carrier in the U.S. with approximately 1.3M members, behind only AARP/UHC at 3.8M. In Michigan, the UHC market report's anonymous #3 carrier has 31,098 members (8.30% of the state's 374,795 total); Mutual of Omaha is a plausible candidate based on national ranking, but this is not confirmed. The national scale means: (a) economies of scale on administration; (b) experienced claims processing; (c) less risk of being closed to new enrollment than tiny carriers.

### Caveats

- **The 5-entity Mutual of Omaha structure creates real confusion.** If you see a "Mutual of Omaha" rate-increase percentage in a news article or rate-filing database, verify which entity it refers to. The Plan G product in Michigan is specifically Omaha Supplemental Insurance Company (NAIC cocode 16537), with a 97.5% loss ratio. The parent Mutual of Omaha Insurance Company has different loss ratios for other product lines.
- **The 0.48 complaint index is calculated at the parent-entity level.** Omaha Supplemental Insurance Co is the specific entity, but NAIC complaint data may be aggregated at the parent level. The "0.48" figure should be interpreted as "the Mutual of Omaha family of companies" rather than Omaha Supplemental specifically.
- **97.5% loss ratio + 8.8% recent increase = carrier is catching up, not falling further behind.** This is the *good* version of "thin margin" — the carrier is pricing close to cost and increasing premiums as needed. But it does mean there's less buffer if claim inflation accelerates.
- **A+ Superior rating is the parent rating.** The Omaha Supplemental Insurance Co entity may have a different rating. Verify on [ratings.ambest.com](https://ratings.ambest.com/) before purchasing.
- **The $220/mo premium is for the Standard tier only.** Mutual of Omaha may offer additional tiers (Preferred, household discount) at different price points not shown in the Medicare.gov data.
