## Things to Verify Before You Sign

1. **Pricing method** — most are attained-age, so premiums will rise each year. Ask for a projection at age 70, 75, and 80.
2. **Household discount** — many carriers (AARP/UHC, BCBS Michigan, Priority Health, Mutual of Omaha, etc.) offer 5–12% off if another household member also has a policy with them.
3. **Rate stability history** — a cheap premium today may not be cheap in 5 years. Look up the carrier's rate increase history in Michigan.
4. **Underwriting tier** — outside your 6-month Medigap Open Enrollment window, insurers can require medical underwriting and decline you. Buy during the window if at all possible.
5. **Tier variants** — (Preferred), (Standard), (Tier 1/2/3) usually reflect underwriting class or household-discount status. Verify which tier you actually qualify for before quoting.
6. **Substandard tier** — carriers list substandard-tier policies (e.g., Bankers Life (Substandard)) for higher-risk applicants; standard applicants pay less.
7. **No standard Medigap networks** — if Medicare approves the service, the Medigap policy generally pays its standardized share regardless of insurer. Provider billing/assignment edge cases can still matter, so verify unusual services before treatment.
